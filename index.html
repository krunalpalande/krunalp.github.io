About

Nook is a learning space that explores design as a method of inquiry, expression, problem-solving, and experimentation. It brings together workshops, experimental sessions, and reading material that move across disciplines—design, pedagogy, storytelling, and critical thinking. At the core of this space is a belief in learning through dialogue. Each session is hands-on, reflective, and adaptable—inviting participants to work with form, ideas, and methods in meaningful ways. Whether you're a student, or professional, this space is designed to build clarity, and nurture your creative confidence. Rooted in experience but always open to experimentation, Nook is less about fixed outcomes and more about cultivating a design practice that is thoughtful, responsive, and evolving.

Learn

Our courses build core skills in design, from structuring information to shaping experiences with interaction, colour, and typography.

Information Design

Learn how to transform raw data into clear, compelling stories. This course covers the anatomy of information, the role of context, and the art of choosing the right visuals. You’ll explore how to frame insights effectively and build narrative visualizations that engage, persuade, and inform.

read more

Interaction Design

Learn how people connect with systems through meaningful interactions. This course explores communication, affordances, and signifiers that shape behavior. Learn to design navigation flows, guide decisions through choice architecture, and craft microinteractions that make experiences intuitive and engaging.

read more

Colour

Explore how colour shapes perception, meaning, and experience. This course covers fundamentals, harmony, cultural context, practical use, accessibility and ethics.

read more

Typography

Learn how typography reinforces meaning, supports reader attention, and upholds professional standards. This course covers the structures and classifications, type selection, legibility, and practical approaches to make text clear, credible, and efficient across media.
