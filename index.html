Nook Studio

about.

Nook is an experimental space. At Nook, we explore design as a method of inquiry, expression, and problem-solving. We do projects, experiments, workshops, and we write and collect material that moves across disciplines. Nook is less about fixed outcomes and more about a thoughtful and evolving design practice.

learn.

We've created a learning space to work with form, ideas, and methods in meaningful ways. Rooted in first principles, we designed this space to build clarity, and nurture creative confidence. Our sessions help build core skills in design, from structuring information to shaping experiences with interaction, colour, and typography.

information Design

Learn how to transform raw data into clear, compelling stories. This course covers the anatomy of information, the role of context, and the art of choosing the right visuals. You’ll explore how to frame insights effectively and build narrative visualizations that engage, persuade, and inform.

interaction Design

Learn how people connect with systems through meaningful interactions. This course explores communication, affordances, and signifiers that shape behavior. Learn to design navigation flows, guide decisions through choice architecture, and craft microinteractions that make experiences intuitive and engaging.

colour

Explore how colour shapes perception, meaning, and experience. This course covers fundamentals, harmony, cultural context, practical use, accessibility and ethics.

typography

Learn how typography reinforces meaning, supports reader attention, and upholds professional standards. This course covers the structures and classifications, type selection, legibility, and practical approaches to make text clear, credible, and efficient across media.

experiments.

These projects are mere explorations, no briefs, no clients, just the joy of making. Each experiment begins with a question, or a visual impulse, and unfolds through play, iteration, and discovery. These projects capture Nook’s spirit of inquiry, treating experimentation not as side work, but as an essential part of design practice.
