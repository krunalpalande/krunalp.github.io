Before You Choose a Colour.

This book treats colour as a decision, one that has consequences for visibility, meaning, and function. Every colour choice does something, whether it is intentional or not. This book is written to help make those choices deliberate.

This book follows the following structure. Before choosing a colour, consider:

# Title
Chapter 01 Dimensions of Colour
Chapter 02 Visibility
Chapter 03 Systems
Chapter 04 Context
Chapter 05 Meaning

These are mere lenses. The aim is not to arrive at the “right” colour, but to arrive at a reasoned one.

Two positions anchor this book. Colour as a system, and colour as a perceptual event.

The first comes from Albert H. Munsell, who treated colour as a describable and navigable system. By separating hue, value, and chroma, Munsell gave designers a way to speak about colour without relying on taste or metaphor. His work reminds us that colour can be measured, compared, and tested.

The second comes from Josef Albers, who demonstrated that colour is never stable. Through simple exercises, Albers showed that the same colour can appear different depending on its surroundings. His work reminds us that colour does not exist in isolation.

You may read this book from cover to cover, or open it when something feels unclear; and refer to a section. This book is work in progress, as I keep adding material to it. Hope this book helps you decide the colours better.

-- Krunal Palande
